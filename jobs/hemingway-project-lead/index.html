123CBT.online — WPA Mental Health Learning Platform

Problem: The World Psychiatric Association needed a multilingual, globally accessible CBT learning platform for its 250,000+ psychiatrist members across 100+ countries — built with limited in-house tech capacity.

What was built: Coordinated end-to-end delivery from requirements gathering through content production, quality assurance, localisation, and global rollout. Managed cross-functional stakeholders including WPA leadership, clinical reviewers, and freelance developers across multiple timezones.

Outcome: Live platform accessed by psychiatrists across 100+ countries. Demonstrated full pipeline ownership — research, production, and distribution under one remit — the same scope this Project Lead role requires.

Animated Psychoeducation Series — Low-Literacy MH Content, PACT

Problem: PACT needed mental health education content for populations with low or no formal education in Pakistan — existing materials assumed literacy levels and cultural contexts that didn't apply.

What was built: Led production of a culturally adapted animated series from scripting through visual development, clinical review coordination, and community distribution. Subsequently submitted to and evaluated in a peer-reviewed feasibility and acceptability study.

Outcome: Published in a Cambridge University Press journal as a feasibility pilot study — validating both the content approach and the LMIC-adapted research methodology. Full concept-to-peer-reviewed-outcome pipeline with no external project manager.

OpenMed-MH — Privacy-First Clinical NLP Infrastructure

Problem: Clinical NLP tools for mental health text are overwhelmingly non-commercial (MentalBERT, MentaLLaMA). No Apache-2.0 licensed stack existed for clinical NER + PII de-identification in mental health.

What was built: Privacy-first clinical NLP infrastructure using only Apache-2.0 stack — clinical named entity recognition and PII de-identification for mental health text. Dual OSS/commercial positioning with day-1 service revenue model.

Outcome: Active prototype. Only open-commercial clinical NLP stack in this niche. Positions for both self-hosted enterprise deals and API service revenue.

Python · HuggingFace Transformers · PyTorch · OpenMed · Clinical NER · PII de-identification
Civic Tech · Bilingual · Community Tool

Lyari 3D Crisis Map — Bilingual Interactive Community Map

Problem: Lyari, Karachi — a dense urban area facing 12 documented crisis issues — had no accessible, bilingual tool for residents or advocates to visualise and engage with local problems.

What was built: Single-file interactive 3D map with English/Urdu labels, 12 severity-coded issue markers, community edit flow with admin moderation, and thematic filtering. No build step — ships as one HTML file.

Outcome: Functional prototype demonstrating civic tech for underserved communities. Dual-language UX built without any external translation service.

MapLibre-GL · GSAP · IndexedDB · Vanilla JS/HTML/CSS · Bilingual UX

Practice-in-a-Box — 90-Day Venture OS for MH Clinicians

Problem: Pakistani mental health clinicians lack the tools, systems, and commercial know-how to build sustainable private practices — leaving patients without access and clinicians without income.

What was built: 90-day operating system for MH clinicians: done-for-you platform (website + booking + reminders + monthly reports) + AI-Ready Practice cohort. Structured around a gates-before-speed validation model with 50+ warm clinician relationships as the launch network.

Outcome: Active validation sprint. Demonstrates ability to design structured programmes, manage cohorts, and ship repeatable systems — directly analogous to producing and distributing a report to a defined audience.
